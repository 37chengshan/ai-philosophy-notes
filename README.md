> Philosophical Understanding and Expression of Death Concepts in Large Language Models: A Multi-dimensional Cross-disciplinary Analysis

## 📋 Overview

This repository documents an interdisciplinary research project investigating how Large Language Models (LLMs) understand and express one of philosophy's most fundamental concepts: **death**.

### Research Focus

- **Primary Question**: How do large language models understand and express the philosophical concept of death?
- **Secondary Questions**: 
  - What systematic differences exist between LLM death understanding and human philosophical traditions?
  - How might these differences influence human cognitive attitudes toward mortality?

## 🎯 Core Hypothesis

Large language models' understanding and expression patterns of death may systematically differ from human philosophical traditions. These differences potentially reflect cultural biases embedded in training data and may influence human cognitive attitudes toward death when interacting with AI systems.

## 📊 Five-Dimensional Analytical Framework

This research adopts a comprehensive five-dimensional approach:

1. **[Conceptual Analysis](research/dimensions/dimension1-conceptual.md)** - How GPT-4 defines and articulates death-related concepts
2. **[Philosophical Tradition Comparison](research/dimensions/dimension2-tradition-comparison.md)** - Alignment with existentialist, Eastern, and religious traditions
3. **[Cultural & Linguistic Variation](research/dimensions/dimension3-cultural-variation.md)** - Cross-cultural bias detection
4. **[Contextual Variation Analysis](research/dimensions/dimension4-contextual.md)** - Medical, literary, philosophical, everyday contexts
5. **[Cognitive & Ethical Implications](research/dimensions/dimension5-cognitive-ethical.md)** - Impact on human death attitudes
